TOPIC: Croup Symptoms and Management
SOURCE: WHO/NHS/CDC (public guidelines)
LANGUAGE: en

Croup is a common respiratory condition in children aged 6 months to 3 years, caused by viral infection of the upper airway.

SYMPTOMS:
- Distinctive "barking" cough (sounds like a seal bark)
- Hoarse voice
- Stridor (harsh, high-pitched sound when breathing in)
- Symptoms often worse at night
- May have mild fever and runny nose initially
- Usually lasts 3-7 days, worst on nights 2-3

SEVERITY LEVELS:

Mild croup:
- Barking cough but no stridor at rest
- Child is active and feeding normally
- No breathing difficulty
- Can be managed at home

Moderate croup:
- Stridor audible at rest
- Some chest indrawing
- Child is restless or agitated
- Requires medical evaluation

Severe croup (EMERGENCY):
- Severe stridor at rest
- Significant breathing difficulty
- Marked chest indrawing
- Pale or blue skin
- Extreme agitation or drowsiness
- Call ambulance immediately

HOME MANAGEMENT (for mild croup):
- Keep child calm (crying worsens symptoms)
- Sit child upright
- Offer fluids frequently
- Cool mist humidifier in room
- Take child outside in cool night air (can provide temporary relief)
- Paracetamol or ibuprofen for fever and discomfort
- Steam from hot shower (parent holds child in steamy bathroom, not in shower) — use with caution

WHEN TO SEE DOCTOR:
- Stridor present when child is calm and at rest
- Breathing difficulty
- Child unable to drink or swallow
- Drooling or difficulty swallowing saliva
- Child appears very unwell
- Symptoms not improving after 48 hours
- Child under 6 months with croup symptoms

MEDICAL TREATMENT:
Doctors may prescribe:
- Oral corticosteroids (dexamethasone) to reduce airway swelling
- Nebulized epinephrine for severe cases
- Hospitalization if severe

DO NOT give cough medicines to young children with croup.
