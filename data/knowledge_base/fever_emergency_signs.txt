TOPIC: Fever Emergency Warning Signs
SOURCE: WHO/NHS/CDC (public guidelines)
LANGUAGE: en

Certain symptoms accompanying fever require immediate emergency medical attention. These are red flag signs that indicate a potentially serious condition.

CALL EMERGENCY SERVICES IMMEDIATELY if a child with fever has any of these:

Neurological signs:
- Seizure or convulsion (body stiffening, jerking movements, loss of consciousness)
- Severe headache that doesn't improve with pain medication
- Stiff neck (child cannot touch chin to chest)
- Confusion, disorientation, or unusual behavior
- Difficulty waking or extreme drowsiness
- Loss of consciousness or unresponsiveness
- Bulging soft spot (fontanelle) in babies

Respiratory signs:
- Difficulty breathing or rapid breathing
- Blue lips, tongue, or face (cyanosis)
- Grunting sounds with breathing
- Severe chest indrawing (ribs pulling in with each breath)
- Wheezing with respiratory distress

Circulatory signs:
- Pale, mottled, or blue skin
- Cold hands and feet despite high fever
- Non-blanching rash (doesn't fade when pressed with glass) — possible meningitis

Severe dehydration:
- No wet diapers for 8+ hours
- Sunken eyes
- Dry mouth and tongue
- No tears when crying
- Extreme lethargy

Other emergency signs:
- Persistent vomiting (cannot keep any fluids down)
- Severe abdominal pain
- Inconsolable crying for hours
- Fever above 40°C (104°F) that doesn't respond to medication

For infants under 3 months: ANY fever is an emergency and requires immediate medical evaluation.

Do not wait or "see how it goes" if any of these signs are present. Time is critical in serious infections.
