TOPIC: Ear Infection Signs in Children
SOURCE: WHO/NHS/CDC (public guidelines)
LANGUAGE: en

Ear infections (otitis media) are very common in young children. The Eustachian tube connecting the middle ear to the throat is shorter and more horizontal in children, making infections more likely.

SYMPTOMS:
- Ear pain (child may pull or tug at ear)
- Crying more than usual, especially when lying down
- Difficulty sleeping
- Fever (may be present but not always)
- Fluid draining from ear
- Difficulty hearing or responding to sounds
- Loss of balance or clumsiness
- Irritability and fussiness
- Loss of appetite (swallowing can cause ear pain)

In babies who cannot tell you their ear hurts:
- Pulling or tugging at ear
- Increased crying, especially at night
- Trouble sleeping
- Fever
- Fluid draining from ear
- Fussiness after a cold

TYPES:

Acute otitis media (middle ear infection):
- Most common type
- Often follows a cold or upper respiratory infection
- Fluid and pus build up behind eardrum
- Causes pain and fever

Otitis media with effusion (glue ear):
- Fluid remains in middle ear after infection clears
- May not cause pain but affects hearing
- Can persist for weeks or months

Otitis externa (swimmer's ear):
- Infection of outer ear canal
- Pain when ear is touched or pulled
- Often follows swimming or water exposure

HOME MANAGEMENT:
- Pain relief: paracetamol or ibuprofen
- Warm compress on affected ear
- Keep child upright (lying flat increases pain)
- Ensure adequate fluids
- Do NOT put anything in the ear canal

WHEN TO SEE DOCTOR:
- Child under 6 months with suspected ear infection
- Severe ear pain
- Symptoms lasting more than 2-3 days
- Fluid, pus, or blood draining from ear
- Hearing loss
- Recurrent ear infections (3+ in 6 months)
- Symptoms not improving with treatment

ANTIBIOTICS:
Not always needed — many ear infections are viral and resolve on their own. Doctor will decide based on age, severity, and symptoms.

PREVENTION:
- Breastfeeding (provides immune protection)
- Avoid smoke exposure
- Keep vaccinations up to date
- Avoid bottle-feeding while lying flat
