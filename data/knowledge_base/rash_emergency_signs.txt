TOPIC: Rash Emergency Warning Signs in Children
SOURCE: WHO/NHS/CDC (public guidelines)
LANGUAGE: en

While most childhood rashes are harmless, certain rash characteristics require urgent or emergency medical attention.

EMERGENCY — CALL AMBULANCE IMMEDIATELY:

Non-blanching rash (Glass Test):
- Press a clear glass firmly against the rash
- If the rash does NOT fade or turn white under pressure, this is a non-blanching rash
- This can be a sign of meningococcal disease or septicemia (blood poisoning)
- This is a medical emergency — do not wait
- Other signs of meningitis: stiff neck, severe headache, sensitivity to light, fever, vomiting

Purpura or Petechiae:
- Small purple or red spots that look like pinpricks under the skin
- Do not fade with pressure
- Can indicate serious blood or infection problems
- Requires immediate emergency evaluation

Anaphylaxis (Severe Allergic Reaction):
- Rash or hives (urticaria) accompanied by:
  - Swelling of face, lips, tongue, or throat
  - Difficulty breathing or swallowing
  - Wheezing
  - Pale or blue skin
  - Sudden collapse or loss of consciousness
- This is a life-threatening emergency — call ambulance immediately
- Use epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) if prescribed

URGENT — SEE DOCTOR SAME DAY:

- Rash with high fever (above 39°C) in a child who looks very unwell
- Rash that is spreading rapidly
- Rash with blistering or skin peeling in large areas
- Rash with severe pain
- Rash around the eyes or mouth
- Rash in a newborn under 3 months
- Rash with joint pain or swelling
- Child with rash who is unusually drowsy or difficult to rouse

MONITOR AT HOME:
- Mild rash without fever in a well-appearing child
- Known eczema flare
- Diaper rash
- Heat rash in hot weather
