TOPIC: Respiratory Emergency Signs in Children
SOURCE: WHO/NHS/CDC (public guidelines)
LANGUAGE: en

Certain respiratory symptoms in children require immediate emergency medical attention. Breathing problems can deteriorate rapidly in young children.

CALL EMERGENCY SERVICES IMMEDIATELY for any of these:

Signs of severe breathing difficulty:
- Child is not breathing or has stopped breathing
- Blue or purple color of lips, tongue, or fingernails (cyanosis)
- Skin pulling in between ribs or at the base of the throat with each breath (severe retractions)
- Nostrils flaring widely with each breath
- Grunting sound with each breath
- Head bobbing with each breath in infants
- Child cannot speak, cry, or make sounds due to breathing difficulty
- Child is sitting upright and leaning forward to breathe (tripod position)

Signs of airway obstruction:
- Sudden onset of choking or gagging
- High-pitched stridor (harsh sound when breathing in) that is severe or worsening
- Child cannot cough, cry, or breathe
- Child's face is turning red then blue

Severe respiratory distress indicators:
- Breathing rate very fast: over 60 breaths/minute in infants, over 40 in toddlers
- Oxygen saturation below 92% (if measured)
- Child is exhausted from the effort of breathing
- Altered consciousness or extreme drowsiness with breathing difficulty

Anaphylaxis with respiratory involvement:
- Wheezing or difficulty breathing after exposure to known allergen
- Throat tightening or swelling
- Combined with hives, swelling, or collapse

URGENT — SEE DOCTOR SAME DAY:
- Persistent wheezing (first episode or known asthma worsening)
- Stridor (harsh breathing sound) that is mild but present at rest
- Breathing faster than normal but child is alert and drinking
- Croup with moderate symptoms (see croup section)
- Fever with rapid breathing in child under 2 years
- Suspected pneumonia (fever, fast breathing, cough, unwell appearance)

Do not give food or water to a child with severe breathing difficulty.
Keep the child calm — anxiety worsens breathing difficulty.
