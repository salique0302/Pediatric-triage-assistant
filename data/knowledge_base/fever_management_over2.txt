TOPIC: Fever Management in Children Over 2 Years
SOURCE: WHO/NHS/CDC (public guidelines)
LANGUAGE: en

Fever in children over 2 years is common and usually a sign that the body is fighting an infection. Most fevers are caused by viral illnesses and resolve on their own.

Definition: A temperature above 38°C (100.4°F) is considered a fever. High fever is generally above 39.5°C (103.1°F).

Home management for children over 2 years:
- Give paracetamol or ibuprofen according to weight-based dosing on the package
- Do not give both at the same time unless advised by a doctor
- Encourage plenty of fluids — water, diluted juice, broth
- Dress in light, comfortable clothing
- Keep the room cool and well-ventilated
- Sponging with lukewarm (not cold) water may help if child is very uncomfortable
- Allow rest but do not force sleep

Fever and activity: A child who has a fever but is alert, drinking, and playing does not necessarily need medication. Treat the child, not the number.

When to see a doctor for children over 2:
- Fever above 40°C (104°F)
- Fever lasting more than 5 days
- Child looks very unwell, is unusually drowsy, or difficult to rouse
- Child has a febrile seizure (convulsion)
- Rash appears alongside fever
- Severe headache or stiff neck
- Ear pain or pulling at ears
- Difficulty breathing
- Child is not drinking and shows signs of dehydration

Return to school or nursery: Children should be fever-free for at least 24 hours before returning.
