TOPIC: Common Rash Types in Children
SOURCE: WHO/NHS/CDC (public guidelines)
LANGUAGE: en

Rashes are very common in children and most are harmless. Understanding the type of rash helps determine the appropriate response.

VIRAL RASHES:

Roseola (Sixth Disease):
- Affects children 6 months to 3 years
- High fever for 3-5 days, then fever breaks and pink-red rash appears on trunk
- Rash is flat or slightly raised, non-itchy
- Child usually feels better once rash appears
- No specific treatment needed; resolves in 1-3 days

Hand, Foot and Mouth Disease:
- Small blisters on hands, feet, and inside mouth
- May have mild fever and sore throat
- Common in children under 5
- Highly contagious; keep child home from school
- Treat symptoms: pain relief, fluids, soft foods

Chickenpox (Varicella):
- Starts as red spots that become fluid-filled blisters
- Very itchy; appears in crops over several days
- Fever and feeling unwell
- Highly contagious until all blisters have crusted over
- Calamine lotion for itching; keep nails short to prevent scratching

Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis):
- Chronic, itchy, dry, inflamed skin
- Common in babies and young children
- Often appears on cheeks, scalp, arms, legs
- Triggered by dry skin, heat, certain soaps, foods
- Manage with regular moisturizing, avoid triggers, use prescribed creams

Heat Rash (Prickly Heat):
- Small red bumps or blisters in skin folds or areas covered by clothing
- Caused by blocked sweat glands in hot weather
- Keep skin cool and dry; loose clothing; no treatment usually needed

Diaper Rash:
- Red, irritated skin in diaper area
- Caused by prolonged contact with wet or soiled diapers
- Keep area clean and dry; apply zinc oxide barrier cream
- Change diapers frequently
