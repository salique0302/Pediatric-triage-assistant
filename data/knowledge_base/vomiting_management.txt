TOPIC: Vomiting Management in Children
SOURCE: WHO/NHS/CDC (public guidelines)
LANGUAGE: en

Vomiting in children is common and usually caused by viral gastroenteritis (stomach flu). Most cases resolve within 24-48 hours.

COMMON CAUSES:
- Viral gastroenteritis (most common)
- Food poisoning
- Motion sickness
- Overeating
- Coughing (can trigger vomiting in young children)
- Ear infection
- Urinary tract infection
- More serious causes are rare but include appendicitis, meningitis, bowel obstruction

HOME MANAGEMENT:

Immediate care after vomiting:
- Wait 30-60 minutes before offering fluids
- Start with small sips of clear fluids (5-10ml every 5-10 minutes)
- Gradually increase amount if tolerated
- Oral rehydration solution (ORS) is best for replacing lost fluids and electrolytes

Reintroducing fluids:
- If child vomits again, wait another hour and restart with smaller amounts
- Once keeping fluids down for 3-4 hours, can try bland foods
- Breastfed babies: continue breastfeeding, offer more frequently in smaller amounts
- Formula-fed babies: continue normal formula

Reintroducing food:
- Start with bland, easy-to-digest foods: crackers, toast, rice, banana
- Avoid dairy, fatty, or spicy foods for 24 hours
- Return to normal diet gradually over 1-2 days

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:

Urgent (same day):
- Vomiting lasting more than 24 hours
- Unable to keep any fluids down for 12+ hours
- Signs of dehydration: dry mouth, no tears, no wet diaper for 6+ hours, sunken eyes
- Vomiting with high fever (above 39°C)
- Severe abdominal pain
- Vomiting green or yellow-green (bile)
- Blood in vomit (red or coffee-ground appearance)
- Recent head injury followed by vomiting
- Child under 3 months vomiting repeatedly

Emergency (call ambulance):
- Severe dehydration: extreme lethargy, unresponsive, sunken fontanelle
- Projectile vomiting in young infant (may indicate pyloric stenosis)
- Vomiting with severe headache and stiff neck
- Vomiting with difficulty breathing
- Abdominal swelling or severe tenderness
