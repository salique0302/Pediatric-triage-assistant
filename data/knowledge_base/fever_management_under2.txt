TOPIC: Fever Management in Children Under 2 Years
SOURCE: WHO/NHS/CDC (public guidelines)
LANGUAGE: en

Fever in infants and young children under 2 years requires careful attention. A fever is generally defined as a rectal temperature of 38°C (100.4°F) or higher.

For infants under 3 months, any fever is considered serious and requires immediate medical evaluation. The immune system is still developing, and infections can progress rapidly.

For children 3-24 months with fever:
- Monitor temperature regularly using a reliable thermometer
- Ensure adequate fluid intake to prevent dehydration
- Dress the child in light clothing
- Keep room temperature comfortable (not too warm)
- Paracetamol can be given from 2 months if needed (correct dosing by weight)
- Ibuprofen can be given from 3 months and over 5kg body weight

Do NOT give aspirin to children under 16 years due to risk of Reye's syndrome.

Signs that require urgent medical attention in young children with fever:
- Fever in baby under 3 months
- Fever above 39°C in baby 3-6 months
- Fever lasting more than 5 days
- Child is unusually drowsy or difficult to wake
- Child refuses to drink or feed
- Reduced wet diapers (sign of dehydration)
- Persistent vomiting
- Severe headache
- Stiff neck
- Rash that doesn't fade when pressed with a glass
- Breathing difficulties
- Child is crying inconsolably

Most fevers in young children are caused by viral infections and will resolve within 3-5 days. However, the younger the child, the more cautious the approach should be.
