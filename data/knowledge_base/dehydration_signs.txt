TOPIC: Dehydration Signs in Children
SOURCE: WHO/NHS/CDC (public guidelines)
LANGUAGE: en

Dehydration occurs when a child loses more fluids than they take in. It is a serious concern in young children, especially with vomiting, diarrhea, or fever.

CAUSES:
- Vomiting and diarrhea (most common)
- Fever (increases fluid loss through sweating)
- Refusing to drink due to illness
- Excessive sweating in hot weather
- Inadequate fluid intake

SIGNS OF MILD TO MODERATE DEHYDRATION:
- Dry mouth and lips
- Decreased urine output (fewer wet diapers)
- Darker yellow urine
- Thirst
- Decreased tears when crying
- Slightly sunken eyes
- Dry skin (less elastic — skin pinch test)
- Irritability or fussiness
- Decreased activity or energy

SIGNS OF SEVERE DEHYDRATION (EMERGENCY):
- Very dry mouth and tongue
- No tears when crying
- Sunken eyes
- Sunken soft spot (fontanelle) on baby's head
- No wet diaper for 8-12 hours (or very little urine)
- Very dark urine or no urine
- Extreme lethargy or drowsiness
- Unresponsive or difficult to wake
- Cold hands and feet
- Rapid breathing
- Rapid heart rate
- Skin very dry and not elastic (pinched skin stays up)

AGE-SPECIFIC INDICATORS:

Babies (under 1 year):
- Fewer than 6 wet diapers in 24 hours
- Sunken fontanelle
- No tears when crying
- Dry mouth

Toddlers and older children:
- No urination for 8+ hours
- Very dark yellow urine
- Extreme thirst or refusal to drink
- Dizziness or lightheadedness

PREVENTION AND TREATMENT:

Mild dehydration:
- Offer oral rehydration solution (ORS) frequently in small amounts
- Continue breastfeeding
- Avoid sugary drinks or undiluted juice
- Offer fluids every 15-30 minutes

Moderate dehydration:
- See doctor same day
- May need ORS in larger amounts
- Monitor urine output

Severe dehydration:
- EMERGENCY — call ambulance
- May require IV fluids in hospital
- Do not delay seeking help

WHEN TO SEE DOCTOR:
- Signs of moderate to severe dehydration
- Unable to keep any fluids down
- Vomiting or diarrhea lasting more than 24 hours
- Baby under 6 months with any dehydration signs
- Blood in vomit or stool
