TOPIC: Common Respiratory Symptoms in Children
SOURCE: WHO/NHS/CDC (public guidelines)
LANGUAGE: en

Respiratory infections are among the most common illnesses in children. Most are mild and resolve without treatment.

COMMON COLD (Upper Respiratory Infection):
- Runny or stuffy nose, sneezing
- Mild cough
- Low-grade fever or no fever
- Sore throat
- Usually lasts 7-10 days
- Treatment: rest, fluids, saline nasal drops, humidifier
- Paracetamol or ibuprofen for discomfort if needed

COUGH:
Types and management:
- Dry cough: often viral, may worsen at night; honey (for children over 1 year) may help
- Wet/productive cough: body clearing mucus; encourage fluids
- Barking cough: see croup section
- Whooping cough: severe coughing fits followed by "whoop" sound; requires medical evaluation

Home care for cough:
- Keep child hydrated
- Elevate head during sleep (for older children)
- Use cool mist humidifier
- Avoid smoke exposure
- Honey (1 teaspoon for children over 1 year) before bed

NASAL CONGESTION:
- Very common in babies and young children
- Makes feeding difficult in infants
- Management:
  - Saline nasal drops or spray
  - Nasal aspirator to clear mucus (especially before feeding)
  - Humidifier in room
  - Keep child upright during and after feeding

SORE THROAT:
- Often accompanies colds
- Can be viral or bacterial (strep throat)
- Management:
  - Soft, cool foods
  - Warm liquids (for older children)
  - Pain relief with paracetamol or ibuprofen
  - See doctor if: severe pain, difficulty swallowing, high fever, white patches on throat

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Difficulty breathing or rapid breathing
- Wheezing
- Cough lasting more than 3 weeks
- Cough with high fever
- Coughing up blood
- Child under 3 months with any respiratory symptoms
- Refusing to drink or eat
- Symptoms worsening after initial improvement
